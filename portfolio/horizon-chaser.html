Horizon Chaser

Realtime WebXR Project
Creative Director, Concept Artist: Apolline Tardy
3D Artist (Texture, Lighting, Animation): Ayushman Johri
Commissioned for a leading tech company's new VR platform (under NDA).
Going from Bennington College to Carnegie Mellon University was like crossing a bridge between two completely different worlds. At Bennington, a liberal arts college nestled in the Vermont mountains, learning was slow, intentional, and deeply connected to nature. My days were filled with quiet discovery—leafing through books under open skies, practicing Chinese calligraphy on chalkboards, and painting landscapes en plein air. In contrast, CMU thrived on a future-oriented culture, speed and technology. I moved from the pages of linguistics books to the interfaces of corpus analysis software and data concordancers. I experimented with platforms like Figma and Adobe Illustrator to expand my artistic vocabulary. At the Entertainment Technology Center (ETC), I discovered a community of artists adapting traditional creative tools to the digital sphere, and what I thought was a leap into the unknown became a bridge.

Of all the artistic fields, Extended Reality (XR) captured my imagination the most. It’s not just about creating art—it’s about stepping into it, and walking inside of an artist’s vision. Freed from the static confines of walls and galleries, XR is like a portable experience that can be accessed anywhere, anytime. I was eager to dive into it, and I knew the best way to learn would be by creating. That’s when I met ETC alumni and Senior 3D Artist Ayushman Johri. Ayushman specializes in stylized realism: he adheres to the physics of reality to create lifelike 3D objects, then bends those rules to elevate their visual impact. His work brings colors to life, with textures so vivid you almost touch them. Watching him carefully draw the worn textures by hand, I was reminded of the slow, deliberate process I follow in my own oil painting. Inspired by our shared approach, we teamed up to create a project we called “Horizon Chaser”.
